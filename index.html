Chopin's solo piano works


Frédéric Chopin (1810-1849) is one of the most renown composers. He who wrote primarily for the solo piano, and during his lifetime, wrote a variety of compositions, ranging from waltzes to polonaises. With all of these compositions, one has to wonder - what was the kind of composition Chopin enjoyed creating the most?

Use the navigational buttons at the top to navigate and learn about Chopin's compositions.


Notes:
  • This is focused on Chopin's solo piano works, so no concertos or pieces with other instruments are included.
  • Some minor modifications were made.
    • All posthumously published works were removed because there were some inconsistencies in year data.
    • If a composition was composed between a time period, the composition date was the average of the two. E.g., a composition written between 1838-1839 would be averaged as 1838.5.
